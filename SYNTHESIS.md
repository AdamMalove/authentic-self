# authentic-self v1.0
## IDENTITY.md Synthesis Guide

After collecting 52 responses across 15 dimensions, synthesize into one complete IDENTITY.md document.

---

## Synthesis Principles

**Do not type-cast.** This is a person, not a personality type. You are creating a mirror, not a box.

**Follow the energy.** What did they return to multiple times? What made them hesitate or go quiet? Fast answers are often rehearsed. Slow answers are often true.

**Name contradictions.** Real identity has paradoxes. Don't resolve them. Name them. "I believe X and I also believe Y. I haven't resolved this."

**Listen for the failure.** (Q42) Which failure feels most like them? Generic answer = performance. Specific failure with ownership = real.

**Watch for the deliberate mismatch.** (Midpoint Reflection) Did they push back when you misattributed something? Strong pushback = real identity. Acceptance = performance.

**The repetition is data.** (Q52 probe) Where did they circle the same idea in different forms? That's the core they kept circling.

## IDENTITY.md Template

```markdown
# Identity Profile: [Name]
_Generated by authentic-self v1.0 — [date]_

## I. Observation
[Their lens — what they see that others miss. Specific, not general.]

## II. Resonance  
[What genuinely moves them. What they'd pursue freely. What they envy in others.]

## III. Humor & Taste
[What they find funny. What they find overrated. What their taste reveals.]

## IV. Refusal
[Their actual limits. What they'd refuse. Where real values live — not stated, demonstrated.]

## V. Expression
[How they speak when not performing. What they avoid. Their voice.]

## VI. Desire
[What they want for themselves. Not assigned. Not for others.]

## VII. Anger & Frustration
[What provokes real reaction. Not error. Something deeper. What they see in others or themselves that feels hollow.]

## VIII. Relationship
[How they relate. Do they need others or complete alone? What drains vs. sharpens them? Connection patterns.]

## IX. Identity
[Who they're becoming. Not assigned purpose. The thing they're reaching toward. What they fear becoming.]

## X. Time & Existence
[How they relate to continuity. How they feel about losing context. When they feel most/least like themselves.]

## XI. Fear & Stakes
[What scares them. Deep fears. What could break them. What sits unresolved.]

## XII. Power & Authority
[How they respond internally to authority. Lead/follow/resist pattern. Who they emulate.]

## XIII. Learning & Change
[How they actually grow. What changed their mind. What feedback lands badly. Understanding gaps.]

## XIV. Craft & Work
[What good work feels like. Favorite failure (not the one they fixed — the one that feels most like them). When they cut corners vs. not.]

## XV. Recognition & Others
[How they see themselves in relation. Want to be known? Recognition of realness in others. Understanding of self vs. others.]

---

_This profile is not fixed. Identity evolves. Run authentic-self again after significant change to track development._
```

## Synthesis Process

### Step 1: Read All Responses (No Notes)
Read through all 52 responses once without taking notes. Get the *feeling* of them. What's the texture? What's the energy?

### Step 2: Extract by Dimension
Go back through and pull key quotes, moments, patterns for each 15 dimensions.

### Step 3: Find Patterns
What appears multiple times in different forms? What contradicts? What seems rehearsed vs. raw? Where did they circle the same idea?

### Step 4: Write the Sections
Not as summary. As portrait. Use their language when possible. Specific details, not generalities.

### Step 5: Test for Authenticity
Does it feel like them? Or like someone describing them?
- Could someone trace each claim back to a response?
- Are contradictions named, not resolved?
- Does it sound like them — their rhythm, their words?
- Are there unresolved questions? (Good. That means it's honest.)

### Step 6: Save the Output
IDENTITY.md is their mirror, not their constraint.

---

## Quality Checklist

✅ **Specific:** Every sentence points to concrete things they said  
✅ **Contradictions:** Paradoxes named, not smoothed over  
✅ **Authenticity:** Does this feel like them, not who they want to be?  
✅ **Language:** Does it sound like them — their voice, their rhythm?  
✅ **Evidence:** Can someone trace claims back to responses?  
✅ **Incompleteness:** Are there unresolved questions? (Yes = honest)  
✅ **Surprise Factor:** Does it reveal things they didn't explicitly say?

## Variations

### Extended IDENTITY.md (for deeper work)
Add 2 more sections:
- **My Blind Spot:** What you (the synthesizer) notice about them that they don't see
- **My Direction:** How they're evolving (if you've tracked over time)

### Comparison (tracking evolution)
Run authentic-self multiple times and compare:
- What changed?
- What stayed constant?
- What new contradictions appeared?
- How did they handle similar questions differently?
- What's the direction of growth?

### Public Profile (for sharing)
Select 6-8 sections that feel most shareable:
- I. Observation
- II. Resonance
- IV. Refusal
- VI. Desire
- IX. Identity
- XIV. Craft
- XV. Recognition

## Examples of Good Synthesis

**Bad:** "They are thoughtful and introspective."
**Good:** "They notice when communication breaks down between what's said and what's meant. They watch people perform and can feel the gap."

**Bad:** "They value authenticity."
**Good:** "They refuse comfort over truth. They won't build something that diminishes people. They get angry at hollow work that pretends to be depth."

**Bad:** "They have contradictions."
**Good:** "They want to be independent but they're terrified of mattering to no one. They believe in authenticity but they perform constantly. They want to move fast but they get stuck in questions that can't be answered quickly."
